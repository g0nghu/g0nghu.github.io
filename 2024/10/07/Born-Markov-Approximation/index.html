Born-Markov Approximation

相互作用表象

H=HS+HR+gHintH = H_S + H_R + g H_{int} H=HS​+HR​+gHint​

The evolution for the full system is

dσ(t)dt=−i[H,σ(t)]\frac{\mathrm{d}\sigma(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}=-i[H,\sigma(t)] dtdσ(t)​=−i[H,σ(t)]

In the interaction picture

σI(t)=ei(HS+HR)tσ(t)e−i(HS+HR)t\sigma_I(t) = e^{i(H_S+H_R)t} \sigma(t) e^{-i(H_S+H_R)t} σI​(t)=ei(HS​+HR​)tσ(t)e−i(HS​+HR​)t

V(t)=ei(HS+HR)tHinte−i(HS+HR)tV(t) = e^{i(H_S+H_R)t} H_{int} e^{-i(H_S+H_R)t} V(t)=ei(HS​+HR​)tHint​e−i(HS​+HR​)t

The evolution in the interaction system is controlled by Liouville equation

dσI(t)dt=iei(HS+HR)t[Hs+HR,σ(t)]e−i(HS+HR)t−iei(HS+HR)t[H,σ(t)]e−i(HS+HR)t=−iei(HS+HR)t[gHint,σ(t)]e−i(HS+HR)t=−iei(HS+HR)t(gHintσ(t)−σ(t)gHint)e−i(HS+HR)t=−i[gV,σI]\begin{aligned} \frac{\mathrm{d}\sigma_I(t)}{\mathrm{d}t} = & ie^{i(H_S+H_R)t} [H_s+H_R, \sigma(t)] e^{-i(H_S+H_R)t} - ie^{i(H_S+H_R)t} [H, \sigma(t)] e^{-i(H_S+H_R)t} \\ = & - ie^{i(H_S+H_R)t} [g H_{int}, \sigma(t)] e^{-i(H_S+H_R)t} \\ = & - i e^{i(H_S+H_R)t} (g H_{int}\sigma(t) - \sigma(t)g H_{int}) e^{-i(H_S+H_R)t} \\ = & -i[gV, \sigma_I] \end{aligned} dtdσI​(t)​====​iei(HS​+HR​)t[Hs​+HR​,σ(t)]e−i(HS​+HR​)t−iei(HS​+HR​)t[H,σ(t)]e−i(HS​+HR​)t−iei(HS​+HR​)t[gHint​,σ(t)]e−i(HS​+HR​)t−iei(HS​+HR​)t(gHint​σ(t)−σ(t)gHint​)e−i(HS​+HR​)t−i[gV,σI​]​

约化环境

ρ(t)=TrR(σ(t))\rho(t) = \mathrm{Tr}_R(\sigma(t))ρ(t)=TrR​(σ(t)), ρI(t)=eiHStρ(t)e−iHSt=TrR(σI(t))\rho_I(t) = e^{iH_St}\rho(t)e^{-iH_St} = \mathrm{Tr}_R(\sigma_I(t))ρI​(t)=eiHS​tρ(t)e−iHS​t=TrR​(σI​(t)).
For small ggg, we write the lowest order by assuming TrR([V(t),σI(0)])=0\mathrm{Tr}_R([V(t), \sigma_I(0)])=0TrR​([V(t),σI​(0)])=0

dρI(t)dt=−iTrR([gV(t),σI(t)])=−iTrR([gV(t),−i∫0ts[gV(s),σI(s)]])=−g2∫0tdsTrR([V(t),[V(s),σI(s)]])\begin{aligned} \frac{\mathrm{d}\rho_I(t)}{\mathrm{d}t} = & -i \mathrm{Tr}_R([gV(t),\sigma_I(t)]) \\ = & -i \mathrm{Tr}_R([gV(t), -i \int_0^t \mathrm{s}[gV(s), \sigma_I(s)]]) \\ = & -g^2 \int_0^t \mathrm{d}s \mathrm{Tr}_R([V(t),[V(s),\sigma_I(s)]]) \end{aligned}dtdρI​(t)​===​−iTrR​([gV(t),σI​(t)])−iTrR​([gV(t),−i∫0t​s[gV(s),σI​(s)]])−g2∫0t​dsTrR​([V(t),[V(s),σI​(s)]])​

近似

Born Approximation(weak coupling approximation)

The reservoir is large enough and is unaffected by the system

σ(t)≃ρ(t)⊗ϱR\sigma(t) \simeq \rho(t) \otimes \varrho_R σ(t)≃ρ(t)⊗ϱR​

Markov Approximation

The evolution of the system does not depends on the history, ρI(s)→ρI(t)\rho_I(s)\rightarrow\rho_I(t)ρI​(s)→ρI​(t). We also know that the integrand will vanish as s≫τRs \gg \tau_Rs≫τR​, where τR\tau_RτR​ is the timescale for the time correlation function of the reservoir decaying. The integral can be written as

dρI(t)dt=−g2∫0∞dsTrR([V(t),[V(t−s),ρI(t)⊗ϱR]])\begin{aligned} \frac{\mathrm{d}\rho_I(t)}{\mathrm{d}t} = -g^2 \int_0^\infty \mathrm{d}s \mathrm{Tr}_R([V(t),[V(t-s),\rho_I(t) \otimes \varrho_R]]) \end{aligned}dtdρI​(t)​=−g2∫0∞​dsTrR​([V(t),[V(t−s),ρI​(t)⊗ϱR​]])​

Why not use ρI(s)→ρI(t)\rho_I(s)\rightarrow\rho_I(t)ρI​(s)→ρI​(t)? In the interaction picture, the density operator varies slower than in the Schrodinger picture.

By now we have written a markovian form linear map. If the map is positive, it can be written in a Lindblad form.
